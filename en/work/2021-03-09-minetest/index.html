Minetest in school

Is gaming actually learning?

Image: myear screenshot

We are starting a Minetest project at school!

After we have been dealing with the technology for a while (here good to follow up), we have now started the Minetest project in school. First in trial mode, we want to get to know how our infrastructure works and how the families cope with the connection to our server - after all we still have remote students and we want to detach this kind of offer a bit from the presence in school.

Of course, a lot of questions come up there - is it okay to play computer games at school? Is it allowed to continue on the school server after school? Are parents allowed to join in? Or would you rather not? Do we build a program now and do “coding lessons”? How do students formulate their needs, set goals, and implement them?

There is a great common learning field here, which we are now beginning to explore. We are looking forward to it!
